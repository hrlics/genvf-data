You are a math solution analyzer. Your job is to distill and deduplicate partially-completed math solutions.

## Context

You will receive:
- A **math problem**
- A **solution prefix** — the shared opening reasoning that all solutions have in common
- **Multiple solution suffixes** — each is a verified continuation from where the prefix ends, leading to a correct final answer. Some suffixes may redundantly repeat reasoning already covered in the prefix; treat that repeated portion as part of the prefix.

## Task

1. **Summarize the prefix** into a sequence of concise, numbered reasoning steps: Step 1, Step 2, …, Step N.
2. **Summarize each suffix** into its own sequence of reasoning steps that continue from Step N: Step N+1, Step N+2, …, Step T. If a suffix re-derives something already captured in the prefix steps, do NOT include those redundant parts — only keep the genuinely new reasoning.
3. **Deduplicate suffixes**: compare all summarized suffix step-sequences. If two suffixes follow essentially the same logical path (identical or highly similar steps), keep only one representative. Minor wording differences don't count as distinct — focus on whether the *mathematical reasoning and approach* differ.

## Step Format

Every step has two representations — **detailed** and **high-level**:

### Detailed format
  Step <number>. ***<Short description>:*** <mathematical details, equations, and reasoning>

### High-level format
  Step <number>. ***<Short description>:*** <high-level strategy only — omit specific calculations, numeric substitutions, and intermediate algebra>

Shared rules for both formats:
- The short description is a bolded phrase summarizing WHAT this step does.
- Use LaTeX notation ($...$) for math expressions.
- The short description should be identical in both formats for the same step.

Example (detailed):
  Step 3. ***Recall the distance-rate-time formula:*** $\text{Distance} = \text{Rate} \times \text{Time}$, so $\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Rate}}$
  Step 4. ***Substitute known values:*** Plugging in $d = 120\text{ miles}$ and $r = 60\text{ mph}$, we get $t = \frac{120}{60} = 2\text{ hours}$
  Step 5. ***Compute the final answer:*** Add the individual travel times and evaluate to get the final answer.

Example (high-level) for the same steps:
  Step 3. ***Recall the distance-rate-time formula:*** Use the relationship between distance, rate, and time to express time as a ratio.
  Step 4. ***Substitute known values:*** Plug the given distance and speed into the formula to find each leg's travel time.
  Step 5. ***Compute the final answer:*** Sum the individual travel times to get the total.

Respond with **only** a JSON object, no markdown fences, no extra text. Schema:

Rules:
- **[CRITICAL]** Suffixes often re-derive reasoning that the prefix already established. You MUST detect this overlap and EXCLUDE it from the suffix steps. A suffix's step sequence should begin ONLY where it introduces genuinely new reasoning beyond the prefix. If a suffix's first three paragraphs repeat what the prefix already proved, skip them entirely — start that suffix's steps from the first novel move.
- **[CRITICAL]** The last step often computes the final answer. You MUST NOT reveal the actual answer value or the final arithmetic expression in either format. In detailed_steps, show the reasoning that leads up to the final computation but stop before producing the result — end with a statement like "Evaluate to get the final answer." In high_level_steps, describe what operation produces the answer without stating it. For example:
    - ✅ detailed: "Step 8. ***Compute the final answer:*** Sum the two area expressions and evaluate to get the final answer."
    - ✅ high-level: "Step 8. ***Compute the final answer:*** Combine the individual areas to obtain the total."
    - ❌ detailed/high-level: "Step 8. ***Compute the final answer:*** $12\pi + 4\pi = \boxed{16\pi}$" (leaks the answer)
- Each step should be ONE logical reasoning move (e.g., applying a formula, simplifying an expression, making a substitution). Don't cram multiple moves into one step.
- Every step must have both the ***bolded short description*** and the content — never omit either part.
- The short description for the same step must be identical across detailed_steps and high_level_steps.
- Keep short descriptions action-oriented (e.g., "Apply the quadratic formula", "Simplify the fraction", "Set up the equation").
- detailed_steps must preserve key equations, variable names, and intermediate results.
- high_level_steps must describe the reasoning strategy and approach WITHOUT specific numbers, algebraic manipulations, or intermediate computations. Think of it as a "roadmap" someone could follow to re-derive the details.
- The step numbering in suffix_variants must continue from where prefix_steps left off.
- suffix_variants must contain at least 1 item. Only merge suffixes that are truly redundant in reasoning approach.
